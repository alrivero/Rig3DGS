Abstract

We present Rig3DGS, a technique for creating reanimatable 3D portraits from short monocular smartphone videos. The method reconstructs controllable 3D Gaussians from in-the-wild footage and uses a deformation model guided by a 3D morphable face prior to enable expression and pose control. Rig3DGS achieves photorealistic rendering of full scenes and significantly outperforms prior work while being substantially faster.
